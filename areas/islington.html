Local intelligence

Things that come up on Islington jobs that are different from the rest of London.

  • Listed buildings & conservation areas

    Canonbury, Barnsbury, Highbury New Park, Lloyd Square, Little Italy and Duncan Terrace are all conservation areas with extensive Grade II listings. We never alter external joinery, sash windows or façade ironwork without LBC. Internal handyman work is unrestricted.

  • Congestion Charge in N1 south & EC1

    Islington's south boundary (south of Pentonville Road, all of EC1) sits inside the £15 daily Congestion Charge zone plus ULEZ. We bundle that into the £18 access charge for any booking that touches CC postcodes — never billed twice if you book multiple services on one visit.

  • Upper Street & Highbury Corner traffic

    Saturday market crowds on Chapel Market and Camden Passage and Highbury & Islington station's road layout make midday van access painful. We schedule N1 moves before 09:30 or after 16:00 unless a Sunday slot is available.

  • King's Cross redevelopment access

    Argent's King's Cross estate (Pancras Square, Lewis Cubitt Square, Coal Drops Yard, Granary Square) requires concierge sign-in for every visiting trade. We book in via the building manager and allow 30 extra minutes per visit; loading-bay use needs to be pre-booked through Argent's facilities team.

  • Highbury Fields & Arsenal-day diversions

    Home matchdays at the Emirates Stadium close several N5 and N7 streets from 4 hours before kick-off to 2 hours after. We track Arsenal's home-match calendar and never schedule a removal on matchday into Drayton Park, Gillespie Road or Avenell Road.

  • Clerkenwell live-work studios

    EC1 has a high concentration of live-work studios (former workshops in Clerkenwell Green, Farringdon Road) with mezzanines, exposed-brick walls and concrete floors. Pax wardrobes need toggle-bolt anchors into brick; TV mounts go into mortar joints, never directly into brick face.

Islington FAQ

Can you work in listed Georgian houses in Canonbury and Barnsbury?

Yes — most of Canonbury Square, Barnsbury Square, Lonsdale Square and Cloudesley Square is Grade II listed. We use reversible fixings, work around original sash windows, and route any external work via Listed Building Consent partners.

Do you cover all of Islington's CPZs?

Yes. Our access charge bundles the £15 Congestion Charge (south of Pentonville Road, EC1), ULEZ, and a tradesperson permit. We book bay suspensions on Liverpool Road, Upper Street and Caledonian Road for longer jobs.

Do Hausio handymen restore Georgian sash windows?

Yes — easing, weight-cord replacement, frame repaint in heritage paints, re-puttying. Full sash restorations on Grade II listed go via our heritage joinery partner for LBC compliance.

How quickly can you cover an Angel or King's Cross flat?

Same-day in N1 if booked before 11:00. King's Cross Argent buildings need concierge sign-in (allow 30 extra minutes); loading-bay use is pre-booked via the facilities team.

Do you avoid Arsenal matchdays for moves?

Yes — we never schedule N5/N7 moves on Arsenal home-match days into Drayton Park, Gillespie Road or Avenell Road. Streets close 4 hours before kick-off to 2 hours after.
