Kensington & Chelsea FAQ

Do you work in mews houses and listed Chelsea properties?

Yes — most of Chelsea (SW3) and South Kensington (SW7) is in a conservation area or has Listed status. We use reversible internal fixings, floor protection on period parquet, and lift rather than drag furniture out of period staircases. For Listed Building Consent issues (façade work, external alterations) we route those through our Part-P / surveyor partners, not ourselves.

How do you handle the big mansion blocks (Kensington Court, Albert Hall Mansions)?

Mansion-block moves in W8 and SW7 require porter coordination — most blocks let us book the lift 48 hours ahead, restrict moves to 09:00–16:00 weekdays only (no weekends), and require ground-floor parking permits we arrange via the porter. Our team carries CSCS cards and ID for sign-in.

Are your prices the same as outer London?

Same hourly rates — £22/hr cleaning, £55/hr man-and-van, £65 first hour handyman. The total often runs higher in Kensington & Chelsea because of (a) congestion-charge access (£18 added for SW3, SW7, SW10, W8, W14 inside the central zone), (b) parking permits the borough charges at premium rates, and (c) mansion-block coordination time. We quote everything up front, no surprises.
