Local intelligence

Things that come up on Wandsworth jobs that are different from the rest of London.

  • Battersea Power Station & Nine Elms estate rules

    Every visit needs concierge sign-in, photo ID, and the building manager's lift booking (most demand 24h notice). Loading bays are tightly enforced via Wandsworth's facilities team. We book all of this when you book us; allow 30 extra minutes per visit for sign-in.

  • Clapham Junction triangle

    Lavender Hill, St John's Hill and Falcon Road are congested 07:00–19:00 weekdays. We schedule SW11 moves before 09:00, after 19:00 or — most commonly — on Sundays when CPZ is free and traffic drops. Battersea Park Road has slightly better access during the day if your route allows.

  • Putney Bridge & Lower Richmond Road bus lanes

    Bus-only hours on Putney Bridge and Lower Richmond Road run 07:00–10:00 and 16:00–19:00 weekdays. Vans without bus-lane permits route via Wandsworth Bridge or Hammersmith. We schedule SW15 jobs for 10:30–15:30 or after 19:00 to slot between the restrictions.

  • Northcote Road / Nappy Valley family cycle

    The Northcote Road / Bolingbroke Grove / Wakehurst Road area runs on a school-term and summer-holiday cycle. We're booked solid for back-to-school deep cleans (last week of August) and breakdown moves into bigger homes during the May half-term. Booking a fortnight ahead in those windows is essential.

  • Earlsfield & Southfields Victorian terraces

    SW18 has dense streets of bay-fronted Victorians with shared rear-access alleys. Big-item removals (sofas, fridge-freezers, pianos) often go in via the rear lane rather than the narrow front hall — we always check the rear access first when planning a move.

  • Tooting one-way system

    Tooting Bec / Tooting Broadway one-way layout means a wrong-direction approach adds 15 minutes. We pre-route into SW17 via Mitcham Lane or Trinity Road depending on the destination side; never via Tooting High Street between 07:00 and 11:00.
