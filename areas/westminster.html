Local intelligence

Things that come up on Westminster jobs that are different from the rest of London.

  • Congestion Charge + ULEZ on every visit

    Almost all of Westminster sits inside the £15 daily Congestion Charge zone (07:00–18:00 weekdays, 12:00–18:00 weekends/bank holidays) plus 24/7 ULEZ. Our flat £18 access charge covers both for the day, regardless of how many trips. Every Hausio van assigned to W1, W2, SW1, NW8 and WC2 jobs is a Euro 6 ULEZ-compliant vehicle.

  • Mansion-block lift booking & porter protocols

    Belgravia, Knightsbridge, Mayfair and Maida Vale mansion blocks need 24–48 hour lift booking through the head porter. We coordinate from your booking — provide the porter's direct line and we handle the call, key-handover, lift-wall protection and clean-up requirements.

  • Mayfair mews access constraints

    Bourdon Place, Three Kings Yard, Adam's Row, Mount Street Mews and similar — gates are typically 2.2m wide and the mews itself can be too narrow for a Luton. We use Transit-size vans for these, with two trips if necessary, rather than parking at the entrance and hand-trolleying the contents in.

  • Embassy-area security screenings

    Streets in and around Belgravia (Eaton Square, Belgrave Square, Chester Square) and St James's have embassies that require additional screening for visiting trades — sometimes a Diplomatic Protection Group check on the day. We arrive early to allow for it; for embassy-adjacent residential moves we recommend morning slots.

  • Listed buildings everywhere

    Vast majority of W1 and SW1 buildings are Grade II or Grade II* listed. We never alter façades, external joinery, ironwork, or original internal features without LBC. Internal handyman, cleaning and removals work doesn't trigger LBC; door, sash and external alterations do — we route those via heritage-specialist partners.

  • Marble, parquet & antique flooring

    Most Belgravia and Knightsbridge entrance halls are marble; many Mayfair drawing rooms have antique parquet; Pimlico stuccos use original Edwardian tiles. We use pH-neutral, marble-safe products as standard, never use steam on parquet or antique tile, and lay protection runners on every arrival.
