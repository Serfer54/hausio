Handyman FAQ

How much does a handyman charge per hour in London?

Hausio charges £65 first hour, £50 each additional hour. Half-day £215, full day £395. The rate covers a fully insured handyman with their own toolkit; specialist materials are billed at cost.

Can a Hausio handyman assemble IKEA furniture?

Yes. Rough guide: Malm bed ~45 min, Pax wardrobe 1.5–2h, Hemnes 4-drawer ~30 min, Kallax 4×4 ~25 min. Bring the assembly fittings; we'll bring everything else.

Do you do minor plumbing and electrical work?

Yes — for non-notifiable work. Tap/washer/seat/socket/switch swaps and waste unclogging are routine. Part P electrics and Gas Safe gas work go to a registered specialist (quoted separately).

Can you mount a TV in a London rental?

Yes — up to 65" on plasterboard, brick or concrete. Typically 1–1.5 hours, £85–£110 with bracket. We drill into mortar joints where possible and patch on move-out.

Can I book a handyman for a list of small jobs?

That's our most popular booking. A half-day at £215 usually covers 8–12 small tasks across a 1-bed flat — shelves, blinds, hooks, tap aerators, door alignment, etc.

How quickly can you send a handyman?

Same-week slots usually available across London. Same-day in Zones 1–3 if booked before 11:00 (£15 surcharge). Specialist jobs need 3–5 days' notice.
