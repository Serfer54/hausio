5. Who we share your data with

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with the following categories of recipient, each bound by a written contract meeting UK GDPR Article 28:

  • Our team members and vetted subcontractors assigned to deliver your job — receive only the data they need (name, address, contact number, job details).
  • Netlify, Inc. — website and form hosting (USA; data transferred under the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses and the EU–US Data Privacy Framework).
  • Google LLC / Google Ireland Ltd — Analytics & Tag Manager (USA/EEA; same safeguards as above).
  • Stripe Payments UK Ltd — payment processing.
  • Our accountants and insurers — for bookkeeping, tax returns, and insurance claims.
  • HMRC, the police, courts, or other UK authorities — where we are legally required to disclose.
  • A successor business — if Hausio is sold or merged, in which case your data passes to the new owner under this same policy.

6. International transfers

Where data leaves the UK (mainly to our US-based hosting and analytics providers), we rely on the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses issued by the ICO, combined with participation in the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (UK Extension) where offered by the provider. A copy of the safeguards is available on request.

7. How long we keep your data

  • Booking and invoicing records: 6 years after the tax year in which the job took place (HMRC / Companies Act requirement).
  • Complaints and insurance files: 6 years after closure (Limitation Act 1980).
  • Unsuccessful quote requests (no booking made): 12 months.
  • Marketing contacts: until you unsubscribe, then a suppression record is kept indefinitely to honour your opt-out.
  • Website analytics: 14 months (GA4 default).
  • CCTV / call recordings: we do not routinely operate either; any recording is disclosed before it starts.

8. Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights, free of charge, subject to certain legal conditions:

  • Access — a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — correction of inaccurate data.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten") — deletion where the data is no longer needed, subject to our retention obligations above.
  • Restriction of processing while a dispute is resolved.
  • Data portability — a machine-readable export of data you provided under consent or contract.
  • Objection — to processing we carry out on the basis of legitimate interests, including direct marketing (an absolute right).
  • Withdraw consent at any time — without affecting processing that happened before.
  • Not be subject to solely automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects. We do not carry out such decision-making.

To exercise any of these rights, email hausio.co.uk@proton.me. We will respond within one calendar month. If you are unhappy with our response, you may complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire SK9 5AF — 0303 123 1113.

9. How we keep your data secure

We use HTTPS/TLS across the site, role-based access for our team, two-factor authentication on administrative accounts, encrypted storage with our processors, and a documented breach-response procedure. In the event of a personal data breach likely to cause a risk to your rights, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and you without undue delay, as required by UK GDPR Articles 33–34.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be highlighted on this page and, where appropriate, notified to you by email. The "Effective date" above always shows when the latest version took effect.
